I'm sharing this because the job market is really challenging right now. It can feel discouraging and overwhelming. But if you keep going, reflect on what didn't work, and stay open to learning — you will find the right opportunity. My success rate was 0.32%, but I have no regrets. Every rejection was a lesson.

The loop that worked

No shortcuts. No overnight success. Just positioning → feedback → adjustment → repeat.

  • 01 Get honest about where you are +

    Analysed 5–10 job descriptions, highlighted what I was confident in and what I needed to improve. Realised I sat somewhere between mid-weight and senior — and that ambiguity was hurting my positioning.

  • 02 Test your positioning with the market +

    Applied across seniority levels. Most responses came from senior roles — encouraging, until I got stuck at hiring manager and case study stages. Common feedback: "We went with someone more experienced."

  • 03 Listen to feedback and adjust +

    More than once I heard: not enough experience working closely with engineers. Instead of arguing with it, I accepted it. That acceptance was the real turning point.

  • 04 Fill the gaps intentionally +

    Joined bootcamps, side projects, hackathons. Spoke with more experienced designers. Deliberately targeted roles with close engineer collaboration. The contractor role was a gap-filler, not a fallback.

  • 05 Notice small progress +

    At first, only passed recruiter calls. Then hiring manager interviews. Then case studies. Then cultural fit. One stage at a time — each new milestone confirmed the adjustments were working.

  • 06 Land the role and keep growing +

    Reached final rounds with two companies. One hired me. Still very aware of my gaps — I set development goals and review them regularly with my line manager. The loop doesn't stop at the offer.
